Truss & Rigging Hire Christchurch

Strong truss, safe rigging, done properly.

We carry a good stock of Eurotruss that we use for everything from simple lighting totems to full ground-support structures and flying rigs. If you need to get lights, speakers, or screens up in the air, truss is how it gets done. We design the structure, install it, hang whatever needs hanging, and pack it all down after.

Our crew are trained in working at height and know how to rig safely. This isn't something you want to wing. Every bit of hardware is rated, inspected, and used within its limits. For bigger builds or anything flown from a venue ceiling, we work with structural engineers to make sure the numbers add up.

What We Do

01.

Ground Support

Free-standing truss structures that don't need anything to hang from. Ground support rigs are great for outdoor stages, marquees, and venues without rigging points. We build them to hold lighting, PA, and LED screens.

  • Self-supporting structures
  • Stage roofs and goal posts
  • No venue rigging points needed
02.

Flying Rigs

When the venue has rigging points or a grid, we can fly truss from the ceiling to hang lighting, speakers, and screens. All done with rated motors, shackles, and steel. We handle the load calcs and make sure it's signed off.

  • Chain motors and hoists
  • Rated hardware throughout
  • Engineering sign-off when needed
03.

Totems and Features

Truss totems are a quick way to get lights or speakers up high without a big structure. We also build custom truss shapes for backdrops, entrance arches, and branding frames. Simple to set up and makes a big visual difference.

  • Lighting totems
  • Backdrop frames
  • Custom shapes and sizes

FAQ

What type of truss do you have?

We use Eurotruss, which is an industry-standard aluminium truss system. We carry various lengths and corner pieces so we can build structures in different shapes and sizes. It's rated for hanging speakers, lighting fixtures, LED screens, and other production gear.

Can you fly speakers and lighting?

Yes. We fly PA systems, line arrays, lighting rigs, and LED screens from truss. All flying is done with rated hardware and our crew are trained for working at height. For larger or more complex hangs, we work with structural engineers to make sure everything is signed off.

Do you need a structural engineer for truss?

It depends on the size and type of structure. Ground-supported truss totems and smaller rigs usually don't need engineering sign-off. Larger structures, anything flown from a venue ceiling, or outdoor stage roofs will typically need a structural engineer's approval. We can advise on what's needed and organise the engineering if required.
